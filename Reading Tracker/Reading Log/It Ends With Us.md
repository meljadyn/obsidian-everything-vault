This book surprised me.

I was looking to get out of a reading slump and figured I would try something from Colleen Hoover, since it was available from my library. I expected something charming and forgettable that didn’t dive into much of anything. I’d heard Colleen Hoover’s reputation. Problematic, shallow, all that stuff. I enjoy problematic romances (in my stories anyway), so I figured this would be an easy and shitty read.

I actually genuinely enjoyed this book. I liked how likable Ryle was (for the most part—the early begging *did* put me off) and I liked the justification from the MC, despite going into this explicitly knowing what abuse looked like. By the very end, I didn’t know for sure that she would leave him. I thought it was handled well for its page count. I’ve heard the criticisms that it could have been done better, and sure, I agree.
