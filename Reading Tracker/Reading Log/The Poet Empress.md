Review written several months later, in June 2026.

I really enjoyed this one when I read it. I enjoyed it more than I expected, on any account. I expected a very cliché story. One about a “cruel” prince who was actually misunderstood before the main character got there. I was pleased to find not only a genuinely cruel character, but a complex one as well. A character who was so impossibly cruel and also someone you felt for and understood in a way. You saw how he became who he is and by the end, you’re not entirely sure if you’re hoping for Wei to end him or stay with him. I was really pleased by the complexity this story handled, including Wei slowly growing her own taste for power. 

This novel punched above its weight (more accurately, above my expectations) and I will be trying the next novel this author puts out.

Unfortunately, it’s not aging as beloved in my head, but I look back at the character-work with nothing but fondness.
