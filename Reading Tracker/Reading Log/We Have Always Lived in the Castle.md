(Review written months later, in June 2026)

I enjoyed this book. I’d even say I enjoyed it a lot. It was strange and I was never fully engaged with the characters, but I found it cozy and easy to engage with. The mystery was interesting, and I started to suspect the younger sister for the murder, but wasn’t sure until it was confirmed. It wasn’t groundbreaking as a novel, nor something I’d readily recommend, but it was decent, mysterious, and engaging. It felt more literary than most of what I read and I enjoyed my time with it. While it didn’t do much for the horror mood I was in, I still enjoyed it well enough.
