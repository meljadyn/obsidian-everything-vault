I enjoyed this read. It’s my first autobiography/memoir, so I’m not sure how it ranks compared to others. I found Jeanette’s story interesting and tragic, the way that her mom shaped her view of herself, her body, her career, all so completely by the age of 18. Unfortunately, it’s months later that I’m writing this review, and it didn’t last particularly well for me.
