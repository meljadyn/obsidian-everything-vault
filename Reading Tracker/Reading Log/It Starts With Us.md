I enjoyed this one okay. 

It was a solid read and I liked Atlas’ deal with his brother. I found Ryle to be … a bit flanderized. I’m happy Lily got her happy ending, but Atlas was so perfect that it was frustrating as a reader. While good men are great, this book failed to show us really any tension at all. We didn’t even see healthy arguments really. He didn’t struggle much even with the coparenting with Ryle. 

It was a solid romance that had no huge issues, but it lacked most of what I liked about the first book—the uncertainty, the tension, the push and pull. There was a complexity to emotions in the first book that didn’t really exist in this one, simply because Atlas was the Perfect Dude.
