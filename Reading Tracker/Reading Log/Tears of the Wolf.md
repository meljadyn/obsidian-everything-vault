This is a higher rating than I normally give romantasy, so we’ll see if it lasts. But I was thrilled to read a romantasy in the perspective of a slightly older woman, a divorcée, and someone who had lost a child. The historical elements were wonderfully done (to my unknowledgeable brain) and I really engaged with Brynn. Cenric isn’t my dreamy favourite boy, but their romance was sweet enough and the dogs helped too. I enjoyed the characterwork, more for Brynn than Cenric as I found Cenric fell a little too fast for my preference. Brynn was falling fast too, but Brynn had been missing a kind presence in her life, and her openness to it in that regard felt more realistic. 

I had a very good time with this story and I have every intention with continuing on with the story.
